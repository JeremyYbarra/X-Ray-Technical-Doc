Skull

Skull X-rays may be done to diagnose fractures of the bones of the skull, birth defects, infection, foreign bodies, pituitary tumors, and certain metabolic and endocrine disorders that cause bone defects of the skull.

The most commonly performed skull x-rays are:

  • PA Axial Caldwell
  • Right & Left Lateral
  • AP Axial Towne's
  • SMV Schuller's Method

Chest

Chest X-rays are the most commonly performed X-ray. They can be used to help your doctor determine whether you have heart problems, a collapsed lung, pneumonia, broken ribs, emphysema, cancer or any of several other conditions.

The most commonly performed Chest x-rays are:

  • PA Projection
  • AP Projection
  • Lateral Projection
  • Lordotic View

Spine & Torso

The spine and torso consists of the Cervical, Thoracic, and Lumbar Vertebrae; as well as the rib cage. These X-rays are used to identify fractures as well as various types of vertebral injuries and abnormalities.

The most commonly performed Spine and Torso x-rays are:

    Cervical Spine
  • AP Cervical Spine
  • Open-Mouth Odontoid Projection
  • Lateral Cervical Spine
  • Swimmer's View
    Thoracic Spine
  • AP Thoracic Spine
  • Lateral Thoracic Spine
    Lumbar Spine
  • AP Lumbar Spine
  • Lateral Lumbar Spine
  • L5-S1
    Rib Cage
  • PA Ribs Unilateral
  • Right and Left Oblique

Upper Extremities

X-rays of the upper extremities consist of the Shoulders, Arms, Wrists, Hands, and Fingers. These X-rays are typically used to diagnose fractures, arthritis, and other abnormalities.

The most commonly performed x-rays of the Upper Extremities are:

    Shoulder
  • AP Internal Rotation
  • AP External Rotation
  • Scapular Y-View
    Humerus
  • AP Humerus
  • Lateral Humerus
    Elbow
  • AP Elbow
  • Oblique Elbow
  • Lateral Elbow
    Forearm
  • AP Forearm
  • Lateral Forearm
    Wrist
  • AP Wrist
  • Oblique Wrist
  • Lateral Wrist
    Hand
  • AP Hand
  • Oblique Hand
  • Lateral Hand

Lower Extremities

X-rays of the lower extremities consist of the Hips/Pelvis, Legs, Ankles, Feet, and Toes. These X-rays are typically used to diagnose fractures, arthritis, and other abnormalities.

The most commonly performed x-rays of the Lower Extremities are:

    Hip & Pelvis
  • AP Unilateral Hip
  • AP Pelvis
  • Frog-leg Lateral
    Femur
  • AP Femur
  • Lateral Femur
    Knee
  • AP Knee
  • Sunrise
  • Lateral Knee
    Tibia & Fibula
  • AP Tib/Fib
  • Lateral Tib/Fib
    Ankle
  • AP Ankle
  • Oblique Ankle
  • Lateral Ankle
    Feet
  • AP Feet
  • Oblique Feet
  • Lateral Feet
